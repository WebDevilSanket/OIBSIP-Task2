Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, commonly known as Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and he passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam was a renowned aerospace engineer and played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

He was often referred to as the "People's President" due to his warm and approachable nature. Dr. Kalam was a source of inspiration to millions, especially the youth, and he believed in the power of education and innovation to transform India.
